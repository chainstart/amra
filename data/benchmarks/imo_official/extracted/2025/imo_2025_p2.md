# IMO 2025 Problem 2

- Source: International Mathematical Olympiad official English PDF
- Source PDF: `data/benchmarks/imo_official/problems_pdf_en/IMO-2025-problems-eng.pdf`
- Day: 1
- Points: 7

Let Omega and Gamma be circles with centres M and N, respectively, such that the radius of Omega is less than the radius of Gamma. Suppose circles Omega and Gamma intersect at two distinct points A and B.

Line MN intersects Omega at C and Gamma at D, such that points C, M, N and D lie on the line in that order. Let P be the circumcentre of triangle ACD. Line AP intersects Omega again at E != A. Line AP intersects Gamma again at F != A. Let H be the orthocentre of triangle PMN.

Prove that the line through H parallel to AP is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle BEF.

The orthocentre of a triangle is the point of intersection of its altitudes.
